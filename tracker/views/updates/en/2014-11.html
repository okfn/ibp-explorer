November Update

Overview

  • Of the 240 budget documents monitored across all 30 countries, a total of 152 have now been published. This is a net increase of two from last month.
  • Of the 88 documents not publicly available, 33 were produced but not published; and 55 were not produced at all.
  • 31 budget documents from 20 different countries were published in November. This includes 19 In-Year Reports, two Pre-Budget Statements, five Executive’s Budget Proposals, one Citizens Budget, two Enacted Budgets, one Year-End Report, and one Audit Report. These documents are all available on IBP’s Open Budget Library.

Making progress

  • Fiji has published its Executive’s Budget Proposal for Fiscal Year (FY) 2015. Previously, Fiji had produced an Executive’s Budget Proposal but not made it publicly available.
  • Kenya has published a Year-End Report for FY 2013/14. Previously, Kenya had produced Year-End Report but not made it publicly available.

Work to be done

  • Fiji produced a Pre-Budget Statement for FY 2015 but did not make it publicly available. This is actually a step back as previously Fiji made the Pre-Budget Statement publicly available.

The Month Ahead

  • Many countries included in the OBS Tracker are currently in the budget formulation stage. We will be keeping an eye out for Executive’s Budget Proposals for FY 2015, along with corresponding Citizens Budgets.
  • Executive’s Budget Proposals for FY 2015 have been published in 20 countries. This means six countries have yet to publish this critically important document for the fiscal year starting 1 January 2015.
  • Of these six countries, Bolivia, Iraq, Palestine, and Vietnam each produced an Executive’s Budget Proposal in FY 2014 but did not make it publicly available. Niger and Nigeria both made their Executive’s Budget Proposal documents publicly available in FY 2014.
  • In the coming months, we will also be looking out for Enacted Budgets and Citizens Budgets for FY 2015.

Be sure to visit www.obstracker.org and click on the country or countries you are interested in to see when budget documents are due. All publicly available documents can be accessed through the Open Budget Library (www.openbudgetlibrary.org).

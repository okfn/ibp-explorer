December Update

Overview

  • Of the 240 budget documents monitored across all 30 countries, a total of 153 have now been published. This is an increase of one from last month.
  • Of the 87 documents not publicly available, 34 were produced but not published; and 53 were not produced at all.
  • 23 budget documents from 17 different countries were published in November. This includes 11 In-Year Reports, five Enacted Budgets, five Year-End Reports, and two Audit Reports. These documents are all available on IBP’s Open Budget Library.

Language update

  • The OBS Tracker website is now available in five languages: Arabic, English, French, Russian, and Spanish. You can access these languages by clicking on the language name in the upper right hand corner of the page.

Making progress

  • Niger has published its Audit Report for Fiscal Year (FY) 2013. For the previous fiscal year, Niger had not produced an Audit Report.
  • Tanzania has published a Year-End Report for FY 2013/14. For the previous fiscal year, Tanzania had not produced a Year-End Report.

Work to be done

  • Fiji did not produce a Year-End Report for FY 2013. Previously, Fiji had made the Year-End Report publicly available.
  • Iraq continues to restrict their Executive’s Budget Proposal to internal use only. In late December last year, the government released some information on the proposal to the newspaper Al Sabaah. While this does not meet international standards for making the Executive’s Budget Proposal publicly available, it is a small step in the right direction.

The Month Ahead

  • We continue to expect Enacted Budgets for FY 2015. Once the legislature approves the Enacted Budget, governments have three months to make it publicly available.
  • We will also continue to look out for Citizens Budgets for FY 2015. Citizens Budgets are expected to be published at the same time as the documents they are based on.

Be sure to visit www.obstracker.org and see when budget documents are due in the countries you are interested in.

All publicly available documents can be accessed through the Open Budget Library (www.openbudgetlibrary.org).

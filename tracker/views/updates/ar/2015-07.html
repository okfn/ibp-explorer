July Update

Overview

  • Of the 232 budget documents monitored across all 29 countries, a total of 142 have now been published. This is a net decrease of 1 from last month.
  • Of the 90 documents not publicly available, 38 were produced but not published, 9 were published late, and 43 were not produced at all.
  • 22 budget documents from 14 different countries were published in July. This includes 2 Pre-Budget Statements, 5 Enacted Budgets, 12 In-Year Reports, 2 Mid-Year Reviews, and 1 Audit Report. These documents are all available on IBP’s Open Budget Library.

Making progress

  • Tunisia has published a Mid-Year Review for 2015 and an In-Year Report for Quarter 1 2015. Previously, Tunisia had not produced a Mid-Year Review. This is Tunisia’s first newly publicly available document since the OBS Tracker began monitoring in July 2014.

Work to be Done

  • Iraq has failed to publish the In-Year Report for April 2015, despite producing it for internal use. In-Year Reports should be published within three months of the end of the period that they report on.

The Month Ahead

  • As we head into the later months of the year, we expect to see more Pre-Budget Statements, Executive’s Budget Proposals, and Mid-Year Reviews for the countries that operate on a January-to-December fiscal year.

All publicly available documents can be accessed through the Open Budget Library (www.openbudgetlibrary.org).
